# Väki

## One-line function
The impersonal spirit-crowds inhabiting every substance and place — the
audience every song addresses, and the world's literal-minded executor.

## Ratified facts
- Every substance and place has its väki: blood, iron, fire, the swamp.
- Väki respond to what was actually sung, never to what the singer meant.
- Sloppy rhythm reads as mumbling — the väki barely hear the call.
- Correct rhythm with wrong words is heard clearly as something else: calling
  the väki of blood without instruction leaves them present and undirected.
- Words drifting to nonsense mid-song is audible improvisation before an
  offended audience.

## Open questions
- Do väki of adjacent substances (blood/iron in a wound) contest a call?

## Story utility
Every failure mode in the casting system is the väki taking the singer
literally — tie-ins can dramatize consequences without inventing a
punishment system.
