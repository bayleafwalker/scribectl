# Runolaulu

## One-line function
The world's song tradition and its physics: Kalevala meter in 5/4, sung
antiphonally, built on parallelism.

## Ratified facts
- The world beat is the viisi-iskuinen runolaulu pulse — 5/4, not 4/4.
- Kerto (parallelism) is canonical singing: repeating or paraphrasing the
  previous line buys a line of time and reinforces what was sung, at the
  cost of not advancing the song.
- Singing is antiphonal: esilaulaja sings the line, the world (or the väki
  addressed) answers back — the answer tells the singer whether it took.

## Open questions

## Story utility
Antiphony makes cast feedback diegetic; a strong enemy's counter-song turns
suohon laulaminen into a rhythm duel on the page as well as the screen.
