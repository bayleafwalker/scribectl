# Runosong — Design Seed

## Core premise
A rhythm-driven survival RPG in a Finnish-folklore world that breathes in
runolaulu 5/4. All magic is sung: power over a thing comes from knowing and
singing its origin, so there is no spell list — only what you know about the
world. The player learns the beat digging ditches to work songs before any
magic exists, and later discovers the same principle stops blood and sings
people into swamps.

## Design pillars
- Knowledge is the only spell list; lore-hunting is the loot.
- Harder magic is more rhythmically independent, never merely faster.
- Cast feedback is diegetic music, never a UI popup.

## Primary tensions
- Split attention: one hand holds spatial survival, the other holds the song.
- Freeform expression vs. evaluable performance — constrained improvisation,
  not open composition.

## Tone
Mythic, boggy, matter-of-fact about peril.

## Hard constraints
- The väki respond to what you actually sang, never to what you meant.
- The world pulse is 5/4; reality-bending magic runs polymetric against it.
- Resting is not failing: silence re-entered on a phrase boundary costs
  momentum, never offense.
- Freeform notes quantize to the world pulse with judgment windows;
  unquantized tempo inference is out of scope.
