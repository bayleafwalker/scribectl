# Polymetric Difficulty

## One-line function
Difficulty scales by rhythmic independence from the 5/4 world pulse, never by
BPM inflation.

## Ratified facts
- Ordinary loitsut are phrases inside the world's 5/4.
- Curses and reality-benders run polymetric against it — a 3- or 4-beat loop
  drifting in and out of phase; the player feels the wrongness as hemiola.
- Multitonality scales like a drummer learning polyrhythm: easy mode plays
  the composite pattern on one grid, hard mode holds two independent pulses —
  two genuinely different cognitive skills, the easy one a real stepping
  stone.
- Knowing more virket lengthens a spell's loop before it resolves: the
  one-virke verenseisautus is a two-bar phrase fired under pressure, the
  three-virke version an eight-bar sustained channel.

## Tuning knobs
- Drift rates of enemy loops; assist mode quantizing movement to the beat at
  the bottom of the scale.

## Open questions
- Where does the skill floor land for players who never split hands?

## Design utility
The difficulty dial is diegetic (rhythmic independence, loop length,
guidance fade) instead of an options-menu multiplier.
