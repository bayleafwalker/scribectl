## World spine
A rhythm-driven survival RPG in a Finnish-folklore world that breathes in
runolaulu 5/4. All magic is sung: power over a thing comes from knowing and
singing its origin, so there is no spell list — only what you know about the
world. The player learns the beat digging ditches to work songs before any
magic exists, and later discovers the same principle stops blood and sings
people into swamps.

**Hard constraints**
- The väki respond to what you actually sang, never to what you meant.
- The world pulse is 5/4; reality-bending magic runs polymetric against it.
- Resting is not failing: silence re-entered on a phrase boundary costs
  momentum, never offense.
- Freeform notes quantize to the world pulse with judgment windows;
  unquantized tempo inference is out of scope.

## Canon in scope
### Väki
The impersonal spirit-crowds inhabiting every substance and place — the
audience every song addresses, and the world's literal-minded executor.
**Ratified facts**
- Every substance and place has its väki: blood, iron, fire, the swamp.
- Väki respond to what was actually sung, never to what the singer meant.
- Sloppy rhythm reads as mumbling — the väki barely hear the call.
- Correct rhythm with wrong words is heard clearly as something else: calling
  the väki of blood without instruction leaves them present and undirected.
- Words drifting to nonsense mid-song is audible improvisation before an
  offended audience.

### Syntysanat
Origin-words: power over a thing comes from knowing and reciting its birth —
the load-bearing rule that collapses the magic system and the knowledge
system into one thing.
**Ratified facts**
- Command over a thing requires singing its origin: raudan synty for iron,
  tulen synty for fire.
- There is no spell list; a fragment about the birth of iron is a weapon
  against anything made of iron.
- An incomplete origin leaves a hole in the song: the singer must stay
  silent on the missing beats — rests as difficulty.
- Tietäjä power is knowing more words: additional virket lengthen the loop
  before it resolves, trading cast speed for effect.

### Runolaulu
The world's song tradition and its physics: Kalevala meter in 5/4, sung
antiphonally, built on parallelism.
**Ratified facts**
- The world beat is the viisi-iskuinen runolaulu pulse — 5/4, not 4/4.
- Kerto (parallelism) is canonical singing: repeating or paraphrasing the
  previous line buys a line of time and reinforces what was sung, at the
  cost of not advancing the song.
- Singing is antiphonal: esilaulaja sings the line, the world (or the väki
  addressed) answers back — the answer tells the singer whether it took.

### Freeform Casting Evaluation
Parse what the player actually performed — meter, known anchors, rests — and
have the world respond to the literal utterance, not the intent.
**Ratified facts**
- Casting is utterance evaluation, not chart evaluation: the game parses the
  performed song and the väki respond to exactly that.
- Evaluation is constrained improvisation on a three-layer grammar: (1) does
  the line scan in the meter, (2) which known syntysanat fragments and
  väki-names anchor it, (3) everything between anchors is free and judged
  rhythmically, not semantically.
- Notes quantize to the 5/4 world pulse with judgment windows; there is no
  unquantized tempo inference.
- Falling silent and re-entering on a phrase boundary decays the effect
  toward uncast but gives no offense; breaking meter mid-phrase, or forcing
  wrong syllables to hold the beat, is what curdles a cast.
- Guidance UI is the character's memory of the verse made visible:
  fragmentary knowledge renders as fragmentary notation, and full mastery
  fades the lane entirely.
